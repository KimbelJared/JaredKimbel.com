Vocabulary

Law of Diminishing Returns: Receiving less satisfaction with each additional unit of a good consumed

Law of Demand: All else equal, as price increases, quantity demanded falls, and vice versa.

Law of Supply: All else equal, as price increases, quantity supplied rises, and vice versa.

Market Segmentation: Creating separate markets for a good for different groups of consumers.

Market: An Institution that enables buyers and sellers to interact and transact with one another.

Demand: The amount of a product that buyers are willing and able to purchase over some period at various prices. (As price increases buyers will buy less).

Inferior Good: A good where increasing income results in declining demand.

Complimentary Goods: A negative cross elasticity of demand, a. Goods demand is increased when the price of another is decreased and vice versa.

Normal Good: Called a normal good when increase in income results in increased demand for it.
