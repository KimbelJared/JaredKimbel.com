#Econ102/Chapter/1


Vocabulary

Positive Question: Any question that can be answered with facts.

Normative Question: Any question that is based on opinion.

Economics: Study of how people and firms make decisions give limitations. (Choices)

Scarcity: Having unlimited wants but limited resources.

Productive Efficiency: Least amount put in for the most out. Efficiency over equity.

Pareto Efficiency: Improving satisfaction while NOT making anyone worse off.

Allocative Efficiency: The people that most want something get it. Some people could be worse off.

Opportunity Cost: The value of the most valuable choice not taken.

Tradeoffs: Choosing between multiple things and the cost/benefits between both.


Concepts


Microeconomics vs Macroeconomics

Micro is the study of the decision making by individuals, business, industries, and governments.

Macro is about all or aggregate, the study of broader issues in the economy.


The Eight Key Principles of Economics

  1. Economics is about making choices with limited resources.
  2. When making decisions one must take into account tradeoffs.
  3. Specialization leads to gains for all
  4. People respond to incentives (good or bad)
  5. Thinking on the margin
  6. Markets are generally efficient
  7. Economic growth, low inflation and unemployment do not always coincide.
  8. Institutions and creativity explain the wealth of nations.

Ceteris Paribus

Is elating for “with other things the same”. This assumption is used in economics, where some relevant factors are held constant (monthly income)
